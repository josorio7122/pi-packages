# Cold Email Framework — Templates & Examples

Detailed reference for the cold-email skill. Loaded on-demand when templates are needed.

Source: NextPlay (Ben Lang) — "The Guide to Getting a Job with Cold Email"

## Template 1: The Direct Introduction

Best for: When you have strong, relevant experience and a clear role in mind.

```
Hey [First Name] — I'm [Your Name].

I'm emailing because [specific reason: saw funding, used product, heard from friend].
I'd like to help you [specific goal: build the product, grow the team, scale marketing].

Two things that may be relevant:
• [Accomplishment 1 with specific metric at Company/Project]
• [Accomplishment 2 with specific metric at Company/Project]

Can share more, but these feel most relevant to what you're building.

Worth a chat?
```

Word count: ~70 words. Direct, specific, clear ask.

## Template 2: The Product User

Best for: When you genuinely use and like the company's product.

```
Hey [First Name] — [Product] is so good. Used it [recently/last week] for [specific use case] and was impressed (especially by [specific feature]).

Question: how are you doing on [function: copywriting/engineering/design] at the moment?

Asking because I [do that thing professionally].

Don't want to blast you with a wall of text — re: work background, [brief pointer to portfolio/site]. Happy to share past work if helpful.
```

Word count: ~65 words. Opens with genuine product observation, natural transition to ask.

## Template 3: The Bold & Brief

Best for: When you have an impressive but concise background.

```
Hey, my name's [Your Name].

I understand your time is valuable. I'll only write [three/four] bullet points.

• [Key credential or experience]
• [Core technical skills or expertise area]
• [What you want: intern/work at Company this [season]]

How?
```

Word count: ~40 words. Extremely concise, confident, direct.

## Subject Lines

Keep subject lines simple and direct:
- "Question about [Company]"
- "[Role] — quick question"
- "Hey [Name]"
- "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"
- "[Product] feedback + a question"

Never use:
- Clickbait ("You won't believe...")
- ALL CAPS
- Exclamation marks
- Sales-style subject lines

## Follow-up Templates

### Follow-up 1 (3-5 days after initial email)

```
Hey [Name] — just bumping this up in case it got buried.

[One new piece of info or a different angle on why you'd be valuable.]

Happy to share more if timing works.
```

### Follow-up 2 (5-7 days after follow-up 1) — FINAL

```
Hey [Name] — last note from me on this.

Still very interested in [Company/role]. If timing isn't right now, totally understand.

[Your Name]
```

## Why Great Emails Don't Always Get Replies

Even perfect emails fail sometimes. Reasons beyond your control:
1. **Timing** — what you offer isn't a priority right now
2. **Too busy** — founder didn't see the email
3. **Forgot** — saw it, meant to reply, got distracted (this is why 1-2 follow-ups matter)
4. **Spam filters** — email went to spam
5. **Luck** — sometimes it's just luck

This is why **volume matters**: email 10-30 companies. A few hours of work for potentially life-changing results.

## Checklist Before Sending

- [ ] Under 200 words?
- [ ] Read it aloud — does it sound like YOU, not a robot?
- [ ] Clear, specific ask at the end?
- [ ] At least one concrete example with a number/metric?
- [ ] No buzzwords? (executed, leveraged, synergized, drove impact)
- [ ] Honest personalization? (not fake flattery)
- [ ] Addressed to the right person? (CEO or hiring manager)
- [ ] Subject line is simple and direct?
- [ ] You can back up every claim in the email?
