# Cold Email for Job Search

Write effective cold emails that get replies from founders, CEOs, and hiring managers.

This skill follows the NextPlay cold email framework — optimized for job seekers in tech contacting startups.

## When to Use

- User wants to email a company they want to work at
- User wants to reach out to a founder or hiring manager
- User needs help crafting a cold email for a job opportunity
- User wants to follow up on a previous cold email

## Prerequisites

Before writing, gather from the user:
1. **Target company** — name, what they do, recent news (funding, product launch, etc.)
2. **Target person** — name, role (CEO, hiring manager, etc.)
3. **User's background** — relevant experience, skills, accomplishments with numbers
4. **Desired role** — what the user wants to do at the company
5. **Connection** — how they found the company (friend, news, product user, etc.)

## The Framework

### Mindset
- You CAN email any company — even without a job posting, even if they say specific location only
- Startups make exceptions for great people
- Cold email works for real people — it's not theory
- Competency matters — the email works best when the person has demonstrable skills

### The 3-Part Structure

Every cold email must contain exactly three things:
1. **Who you are** — one sentence, plain language
2. **Why you are reaching out** — specific reason tied to the company
3. **Why they should care** — concrete evidence of your value

### Critical Rules

#### Length: ~200 words MAX
- Founders are busy; nobody reads long unsolicited letters from strangers
- You don't need your entire career history
- Less is more

#### Tone: Plain, human, honest
- Read it aloud — does it sound like a real person?
- NO buzzwords: ❌ "executed on key initiatives", ❌ "drove impact", ❌ "leveraged synergies"
- YES specific results: ✅ "helped increase ARR by $1M in 3 months"

#### Ask: ONE clear, specific action
- ❌ Vague email with no next step
- ✅ "Worth a chat?" or "Can I share more about my work?" or "How are you doing on [function] at the moment?"

#### Specificity: Be concrete, not abstract
- ❌ "I work with early-stage startups" → ✅ "I work with early-stage startups (like X and Y)"
- ❌ "drove results" → ✅ "increased organic traffic by 150% in 6 months"

#### Honesty: No fake personalization
- ❌ "I've been a fan of your company for years!" (if not true)
- ❌ "I saw your post on LinkedIn!" (fake)
- ✅ "I saw you just raised a Series B" (honest, verifiable)
- ✅ "Heard about [Company] from a friend" (genuine)

### Follow-ups
- Send 1-2 follow-ups MAX
- Never be spammy
- Space follow-ups 3-5 business days apart
- Keep follow-ups even shorter than the original

### Volume Strategy
- Even a perfect email might not get a reply (timing, spam filters, busy founder)
- Email 10-30 companies to maximize chances
- A few hours of work for potentially life-changing results

## Process for Writing

1. **Gather info** from user (see Prerequisites)
2. **Draft** using the 3-part structure
3. **Check** against all Critical Rules
4. **Read aloud** — does it sound human?
5. **Trim** — cut anything that doesn't add value
6. **Verify** — under 200 words? Clear ask? Specific examples? Honest?

## Finding Contact Info

1. **Guess first**: Usually `firstname@company.com`
2. **LinkedIn**: Find the CEO or hiring manager
3. **Tools** (if guessing fails): Nymeria, ContactOut, Clay
4. **When in doubt**: Email the CEO (safer bet than trying to find the exact hiring manager)

## Rules

- NEVER write emails longer than 200 words
- NEVER use buzzwords or corporate language
- NEVER include fake personalization
- ALWAYS include a clear, specific ask
- ALWAYS use concrete examples with numbers
- ALWAYS read the output aloud mentally — if it sounds robotic, rewrite
- ALWAYS ask user for context before writing (don't assume)
- Present the email in a clean format the user can copy-paste
- If user provides a job description, reference specific things from it
- If user mentions they use the company's product, lead with genuine product feedback
