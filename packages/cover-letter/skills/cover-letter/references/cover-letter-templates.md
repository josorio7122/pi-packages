# Cover Letter Templates

Detailed reference for the cover-letter skill. Loaded on-demand when templates or examples are needed.

Sources: Liz Ryan (Forbes), YC (Ryan Choi), Austin Belcak (Cultivated Culture), DHH (Basecamp), a16z Speedrun.

---

## Pain Letter Template (Liz Ryan / Forbes)

**When to use:** Best conversion rate (~25% callback). Ideal for hiring managers at companies you've researched. Mail physical copy when possible.

```
[Hiring Manager First Name],

[Hook — timely company accomplishment, <6 months old.]
I was glad to see [Company] [accomplishment — e.g., "named to the Inc. 5000 list this year" / "just launched [Product feature]" / "closed your Series B"].

[Pain Hypothesis — educated guess about their challenge.]
Given [context — e.g., "your rapid growth"], I imagine [team] must be [challenge — e.g., "under pressure to [specific pressure]"].

[Dragon-Slaying Story — proof you solved the same problem, with numbers.]
At [Previous Company], I [what you did]. [How you did it.] The result: [specific metric — e.g., "cut time-to-deploy from X to Y" / "grew X by Z%"].

[Soft CTA — low friction, no pressure.]
If you have a moment to chat about [topic], I'd be glad to connect.

[Your Name]
[Phone / Email / LinkedIn]
```

### Worked Example

```
Sarah,

I was glad to see Finch Health named Austin's fastest-growing health tech startup this quarter.

Given the pace you're scaling, I imagine your data engineering team must be feeling pressure to keep data pipelines reliable while shipping new product features.

At Stripe, I took our data infrastructure from 3 engineers to a 12-person team. I redesigned the ingestion pipeline, which cut data latency from 6 hours to 20 minutes and enabled the analytics team to run real-time dashboards for the first time. Revenue attribution improved 40%.

If you have a moment to chat about scaling your data infrastructure, I'd be glad to connect.

Marcus Lee
marcus@email.com | linkedin.com/in/marcuslee
```

## Email Cover Letter Template (3 paragraphs)

**When to use:** Standard job applications via email or portal. Under 200 words.

```
Subject: [Role] — [Your Name]

Hi [First Name],

[Para 1 — Why THIS company. One specific thing: product feature, blog post, funding, decision.]
[Specific observation about the company. Make it clear you did real research.]

[Para 2 — Why YOU. 2–3 quantified results matching the role.]
• [Achievement 1 with metric]
• [Achievement 2 with metric]
[Optional: one example of initiative beyond the resume.]

[Para 3 — The Ask. Clear and direct.]
I'd love to learn more about the [Role] role and share how I can contribute. Available for a call any day next week.

[Your Name]
[LinkedIn / Portfolio / GitHub]
```

### Worked Example

```
Subject: Senior Backend Engineer — Priya Mehta

Hi Jordan,

I've been following Lattice's shift toward skills-based performance tracking since your October product update — it's a problem I've seen firsthand at every mid-size company I've worked at.

At my last two roles:
• Rebuilt a legacy API at Gusto that cut p99 latency from 1.2s to 180ms, directly unblocking 3 dependent teams
• Led a 4-engineer squad that shipped a self-service data export tool used by 2,000+ customers in Q1

I'd love to chat about the Senior Backend Engineer role and how I can help scale Lattice's core platform. Available any day next week.

Priya Mehta
github.com/priyamehta | linkedin.com/in/priyamehta
```

## LinkedIn Message Template

**When to use:** Reaching a hiring manager or recruiter on LinkedIn. Under 100 words. Brevity is mandatory.

```
Hi [First Name] — I applied for [Role] and wanted to put a face to the name.

[One specific reason you're interested — product, mission, or recent company event.]

Quick background: [2 sentences max — most relevant experience + one metric.]

Happy to share more if helpful. Thanks for your time.
```

### Worked Example

```
Hi Jordan — I applied for the Senior Backend Engineer role and wanted to put a face to the name.

I've been a Lattice user and have been closely watching the skills-based performance tracking direction — it's exactly the problem space I want to work in.

Background: 6 years in backend engineering, most recently at Gusto where I led a team that cut API latency by 85%.

Happy to share more if helpful. Thanks for your time.
```

## Formal Cover Letter Template

**When to use:** Explicitly requested in the job posting, or for non-tech / traditional industries. One page max.

```
[Date]

[Hiring Manager Name]
[Title]
[Company]

Dear [First Name],

[Opening paragraph — Hook + clear statement of intent.]
[Specific company accomplishment or observation.] I'm writing to apply for the [Role] position at [Company].

[Body paragraph 1 — Your most relevant experience with results.]
In my [X years] at [Company], I [what you did and how]. This resulted in [measurable outcome].

[Body paragraph 2 — Second evidence point + company-specific fit.]
[Accomplishment 2 with metric.] I'm particularly drawn to [Company]'s [specific product/mission/approach] because [genuine reason tied to your background].

[Closing paragraph — Clear ask.]
I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how my background in [area] can contribute to [Company]'s goals. Please feel free to reach me at [email] or [phone].

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

## Follow-up Templates

### Follow-up 1 — 1 week after applying

```
Subject: Re: [Role] — [Your Name]

Hi [First Name] — just following up on my application for [Role].

[One new piece of context: a relevant link, a new accomplishment, or a fresh angle on your fit.]

Happy to share more if timing works.

[Your Name]
```

### Follow-up 2 — 2 weeks after applying (final)

```
Subject: Re: [Role] — [Your Name]

Hi [First Name] — last note from me on this.

Still very interested in [Role] at [Company]. If timing isn't right now, totally understand.

[Your Name]
```

**Rules:** Max 2 follow-ups. Space them 5–7 days apart. Never apologize for following up.

## Subject Line Examples

**Do:**
- `Senior Engineer — Priya Mehta`
- `[Role] application — [Your Name]`
- `[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out`
- `Question about [Company]'s [specific team or product]`

**Don't:**
- `Exciting opportunity to connect!!`
- `MY APPLICATION FOR THE POSITION OF...`
- `Following up on my application (2nd attempt)`
- `Re: Re: Re: [Role]`

## W-H-I Evidence Formatter (YC Ryan Choi)

Use this to structure every achievement before dropping it into a letter.

```
What:   [What product/project/system did you work on? Describe it in plain terms.]
How:    [What did you specifically do? What tech, team size, cross-functional partners?]
Impact: [What measurable business result followed? %, $, time, users, rank.]
```

**Before (weak):**
> Improved deployment process and reduced bugs.

**After (W-H-I):**
> Rebuilt the CI/CD pipeline for a 12-engineer team at Stripe (What). Introduced parallel test runners and automated rollback triggers (How). Cut deploy time from 4 hours to 15 minutes; production incidents dropped 60% (Impact).

## Portfolio Proof Template (Tier 1)

**When to use:** You built something relevant to the company — highest conversion rate.

```
Subject: Built something for [Company] — [Your Name]

Hi [First Name],

I'm applying for [Role] and wanted to show rather than tell.

I spent [X hours] building [what you built] — [one sentence on why it's relevant to them].

[Link to live demo or GitHub]

A few decisions I made and why:
• [Decision 1 — tradeoff you considered]
• [Decision 2 — why you chose this approach]
• [Decision 3 — what you'd do differently with more time]

Happy to walk through it live. Available for a call any day next week.

[Your Name]
[Portfolio / GitHub / LinkedIn]
```

### Worked Example

```
Subject: Built a Lattice skills gap analyzer — Priya Mehta

Hi Jordan,

I'm applying for the Senior Backend Engineer role and wanted to show rather than tell.

I spent 4 hours building a prototype skills gap analyzer using Lattice's public API — it clusters employee skill ratings and surfaces the top 3 gaps per team.

github.com/priyamehta/lattice-skills-gap

A few decisions:
• Used K-means clustering over manual thresholds — more robust as data scales
• Built the API layer to be read-only so it can run against production safely
• Skipped auth for now — would add OAuth in a real integration

Happy to walk through it. Available any day next week.

Priya
```

## Checklist Before Sending

- [ ] Under 200 words (email) or 1 page (formal)?
- [ ] Addressed to a named person — not "Dear Hiring Manager"?
- [ ] Opened with something specific about the company (<6 months old)?
- [ ] At least one quantified result included?
- [ ] Ends with a clear, specific ask?
- [ ] No buzzwords (passionate, driven, synergy, leverage, executed)?
- [ ] Read aloud — does it sound like a human, not a robot?
- [ ] Every claim is backed by something you can verify in an interview?
- [ ] If a product user, did you lead with genuine product feedback?
- [ ] If a referral, did you mention their name?
